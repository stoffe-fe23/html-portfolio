Web

I have been involved in creating a number of smaller web sites for various of organisations.

I mostly focused on the back-end functionality, such as creating simple custom booking and reservation systems, event registration systems and information databases. I have also created visual themes for the CMS used by the sites.

I worked with the Drupal CMS as a base, using PHP, MySQL and MariaDB as building blocks.

Translation

I have worked translating texts of different kinds and lengths from Swedish into English, or vice versa. Mostly, but not only, for use on web sites. This work also included proof-reading texts.

The most complex translation work I have been involved in is translating the 360 pages book "Vikingakungens guldskatt" by Sven Rosborn into English.

Games

There is more to a creative life than just work.

I have always enjoyed spending my relaxation time playing computer games. Many games I play also tend to tickle my creative itch, inspiring me to create mods for them to better suit my personal tastes or add things I found missing.

I have created numerous game mods over the years, as well as a few modding tools and utilities when such were not available. Most for my own use, but a few released into the wild.

My skills

HTML

The basic building block of the World Wide Web, HTML has gone through many mutations over the years but remains an essential component for building web pages.

CSS

Defining the layout and design of the web, CSS has allowed web pages to come a long way from their humble beginning as plain text with simple formatting to complete user interfaces.

Javascript

Growing the web page from a mere document with structured information into a full fledged application, Javascript is a core component for making your page do whatever you want.

Backend

Being able to store, retrieve and process data on the server side is useful when building web applications. As such I know my way around the PHP scripting language and SQL databases, with some experience with the Drupal CMS.

Benji doing his thing

On a completely random note...

My parents visited and brought their dog Benji with them. The very cute border collie quickly found himself at home on my couch, even though this was his first visit to my place.

Fancying himself something of a couch connoisseur Benji tends to try out the seating furniture of anyone he visits. Being cute and charming, and knowing it, he gets away with it too, mostly.

Adopted from a dog shelter with an unknown but seemingly troubled past it is nice to see the timid dog regain some measure of self confidence and find himself at home in his new family.
